\section{Limitations and Future Work}
\label{sec:limitations}
A big limitation appears from attribute-specific articulation patterns: Certain attributes apply exclusively to individual letters, such as \texttt{Istitala} for (ض) and \texttt{Tikrar} for (ر). Consequently, we expect our model will be unable to capture instances of (ض) without \texttt{Istitala} or (ر) without \texttt{Tikrar}. This limitation similarly applies to Tajweed rules that occur less frequently in the Holy Quran, such as \texttt{Imala}, \texttt{Rawm}, and \texttt{Tasheel}. The obvious solution is to annotate real data with these errors to make the model understand better these errors.

Additionally, the training dataset is composed exclusively of recitations from expert male reciters, which may limit the model's generalizability to a broader range of vocal characteristics. Furthermore, the current scope is restricted to the Hafs recitation style; future work will focus on extending support to other recitation styles (Riwayah), such as Warsh.
